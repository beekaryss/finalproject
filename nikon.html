Nikon Corporation (Japanese 株式会社 ニ コ ン kabushiki gaysa nikon, pronounced “Nikon” (inf.)) Is a Japanese company specializing in the optical-mechanical and electronic production of devices for image registration, mainly cameras and lenses. One of the world's largest leaders in the development of optical devices and the manufacture of optical glass.

Nikon is part of the Mitsubishi Group. Founded by Kōgaku Kōgyō on July 25, 1917 under the name Nippon Kogaku K.K. (Nippon Kogaku Kabushiki Kaisha), renamed in 1988. President - Kazuo Ushida. Headquarters in Tokyo.

Nikon
Nikon Corporation was founded in 1917 by the merger of three optical equipment manufacturers (Seisakusho, Iwaki Glass Co, Fujii Lens) into a fully integrated company called Nippon Kogaku Kokyo K.K. The company produced optical instruments for the Japanese navy.
